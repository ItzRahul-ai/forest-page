🌳 About Forests

Forests are one of the most important ecosystems on our planet. They cover about 31% of the Earth's land area and are home to more than 80% of the world's land-based species of animals, plants, and fungi.

🟢 What Is a Forest?

A forest is a large area covered chiefly with trees and undergrowth. But it’s much more than just trees — it's a living, breathing environment filled with life, sounds, and natural beauty. Forests provide shelter to wildlife, maintain the global climate, and supply oxygen.

🌍 Why Forests Matter

Forests are vital to life on Earth:

  • They produce oxygen and clean the air we breathe.
  • They store carbon, helping to reduce the impact of climate change.
  • They protect biodiversity, offering habitat to millions of species.
  • They provide resources such as food, medicine, and materials.

🌱 Types of Forests

There are several types of forests around the world:

  • Tropical Rainforests – Warm and wet all year, rich in plant and animal life (e.g., the Amazon).
  • Temperate Forests – Found in regions with four seasons, like North America and Europe.
  • Boreal Forests (Taiga) – Cold forests found in northern regions, mostly made up of coniferous trees.

🌿 Fun Facts

  • Forests can influence rainfall and temperature across entire continents.
  • A single mature tree can absorb over 20 kilograms of carbon dioxide per year.
  • The Amazon Rainforest alone produces 20% of the world's oxygen.

🌎 Forests and Us

Forests aren’t just wilderness; they’re connected to human life. Many communities live in and depend on forests for their livelihoods. Around the world, forests also provide spiritual, recreational, and cultural value.
